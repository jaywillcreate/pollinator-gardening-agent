Watercolor Brush Stroke Techniques in SVG

Three core techniques for realistic watercolor brush strokes: (1) tapered filled shapes with gradient masks for soft transparent edges, (2) feTurbulence displacement for organic wobble, (3) layered overlapping strokes for pigment buildup. All using color-matched strokes — no black.

1. Gradient-Masked Brush Strokes — Soft Transparent Edges

A filled tapered shape + a radial gradient mask = paint stroke that's opaque in the center and fades to transparent at the edges, like real watercolor on paper.

Purple stroke — gradient masked Opaque center fading to transparent edges like wet paint
Red stroke — gradient masked Wider center, tapered ends, soft fade at all edges
Gold stroke — gradient masked Warm gold with transparent feathered edges

2. Turbulence-Displaced Brush Strokes — Organic Wobble

Adding feTurbulence + feDisplacementMap makes the crisp brush shapes look hand-painted with irregular organic edges.

Purple — masked + turbulence wobble Same stroke shape but with organic paint-edge distortion
Green — masked + turbulence wobble Foliage-colored brush stroke with natural paint edge

3. Layered Overlapping Strokes — Pigment Buildup

Multiple semi-transparent strokes overlapping creates rich color where they cross, like real watercolor paint layers drying. Each stroke has gradient mask + turbulence.

4 purple strokes layered Each semi-transparent — overlap areas build richer color like real watercolor pigment
Green + red layered (Bee Balm) Green foliage wash underneath, 3 red bloom strokes on top — color mixing where they overlap

4. Full Botanical Swatch — Brush Stroke Build-Up

Complete plant swatch built entirely from overlapping masked watercolor brush strokes. No flat shapes — every element is a painted stroke with feathered edges.

Echinacea — Full Brush Stroke Built entirely from tapered brush-stroke shapes with turbulence wobble. Green foliage strokes + 12 purple petal strokes + brown cone. No flat shapes, no black lines.
Rudbeckia — Full Brush Stroke 14 golden tapered petal strokes + very dark center dome + green foliage washes. All brush-stroke technique, no flat shapes.

5. Thin Brush Strokes — Fine Detail Technique

Narrower, finer tapered strokes for delicate detail — leaf veins, petal edges, grass blades, stem lines. Same gradient mask + turbulence technique but with much thinner shapes (2-6px wide vs 20-40px). Great for layering ON TOP of wider washes.

Hairline to medium — each stroke tapers from nothing to ~5px and back
Main vein + side branches + sub-veins, all tapered fills with wobble

6. Thin Strokes Applied — Botanical Detail Overlay

Layering thin brush strokes ON TOP of wide watercolor washes to add botanical detail — petal edges, leaf veins, grass blades — all in color-matched thin tapered shapes.

Echinacea — Wide + Thin Layers Wide watercolor washes underneath, thin tapered petal strokes + leaf vein strokes on top. Two weights of brush creating depth.
Little Bluestem — Thin Blade Strokes 16 thin tapered grass blade shapes radiating from base. 3 weights: main blades, sub-blades, ultra-thin wisps. Tan seed heads at tips.
