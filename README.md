🌿 Native Plants & Pollinator Garden Assistant — Knowledge Base
A curated collection of authoritative documents powering an AI-assisted gardening advisor focused on native plant selection, pollinator habitat design, prairie restoration, and ecological landscaping across North America.

📖 Overview
This knowledge base is the foundation for a Claude-powered assistant that answers gardening questions with accuracy drawn from expert sources — including nursery guides, academic research, Xerces Society pollinator guides, USDA ecoregion handbooks, and more.
Whether you're planning a backyard pollinator garden, restoring a prairie, selecting region-specific natives, or learning about seed starting and site prep, this repository contains the reference material to support well-informed, ecologically grounded answers.

💡 What This Assistant Can Help With

Plant selection — region-specific native plants for sun/shade, dry/wet soil, bloom season
Pollinator support — which plants attract bees, butterflies, hummingbirds, and beneficial insects
Garden design — border layouts, seed mix choices, companion planting, garden kits
Prairie & meadow restoration — site prep, seeding rates, timing, weed management
Seed starting — germination codes, stratification methods, sowing depth and timing
Cultivar guidance — wild-type vs. cultivar considerations and their pollinator value
Urban gardening — vertical gardens, small-space natives, container planting
Research-backed habitat advice — mowing frequency, lawn alternatives, patch size, pesticide reduction
Ecoregion identification — matching plants to your specific ecological province


🌍 Geographic Coverage
This knowledge base has strong coverage for gardens across the continental United States, with guides available for every major region — from the Northeast and Mid-Atlantic to the Pacific Coast, Rocky Mountains, Great Plains, Desert Southwest, Southeast, and Gulf Coast. Alaska and Hawaii are also included via ecoregion guides.
